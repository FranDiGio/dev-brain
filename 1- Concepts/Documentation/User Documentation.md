### `Definition`

**User Documentation** is a type of **product documentation** that explains how **end users** can **install, operate, and troubleshoot** a software product. It translates technical details into **clear, accessible instructions** for non-technical audiences, helping users achieve their goals efficiently.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Purpose:**  
    To make the software **understandable and usable** for its intended audience.  
    It bridges the gap between complex system functionality and user interaction.
    
- **Audience:**
    
    - End-users (technical or non-technical)
    - Customers and clients
    - Support and training teams
    
- **Content and Structure:**
    
    - **Overview:** Introduces the softwareâ€™s purpose and main features.
    - **Installation Guides:** Step-by-step instructions to set up or configure the application.
    - **Usage Instructions:** Explains how to perform key actions or workflows.
    - **Troubleshooting Section:** Common issues, error messages, and their resolutions.
    - **FAQs:** Answers to recurring user questions.
    - **Visual Aids:** Screenshots, diagrams, or tutorial videos for clarity and engagement.
    
- **Formats:**
    
    - Online knowledge bases or wikis
    - PDF or printed manuals
    - In-app tooltips, tutorials, or onboarding screens
    - Video walkthroughs or interactive demos
    
- **Maintenance:**  
    User documentation must evolve alongside the software.  
    Every new release, UI redesign, or feature update should trigger a review to ensure accuracy.
    
- **Best Practices:**
    
    - Use **simple, task-oriented language**.
    - Include **examples** and visuals.
    - Provide **searchability** and **cross-references** for large systems.
    - Keep it consistent with the **current version** of the product.
