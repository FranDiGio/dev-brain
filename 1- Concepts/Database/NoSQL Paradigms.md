
### `Definition`

**NoSQL Paradigms** refer to a group of non-relational database design approaches that differ from the traditional relational model. They encompass alternative structural philosophies optimized for scalability, flexibility, and distributed system requirements.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- **Non-Relational Orientation**  
    NoSQL paradigms do not rely primarily on fixed relational tables and joins.
    
- **Model Diversity**  
    Includes document, key-value, graph, and other non-relational models, each suited for different data shapes and workloads.
    
- **Schema Flexibility**  
    Many NoSQL systems allow dynamic or loosely enforced schemas.
    
- **Horizontal Scalability Focus**  
    Often designed for distributed environments where data is partitioned across multiple nodes.
    
- **Consistency Trade-Offs**  
    Some NoSQL systems relax strong consistency guarantees to improve availability or performance.
    
- **Paradigm-Level Concept**  
    The term describes structural approaches, not specific products or query languages.
