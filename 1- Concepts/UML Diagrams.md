
### `Definition`

UML diagrams are standardized visual models used to represent the structure and behavior of a software system. They help communicate design decisions clearly using a universal notation understood across the industry.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- Provide both **structural** and **behavioral** views of a system.
    
- Structural diagrams describe components, classes, and relationships.
    
- Behavioral diagrams show interactions and flows over time.
    
- Useful for system design, communication, and documentation.
    
- Common in SDDs and architectural planning.
