
### `Definition`

A Software Design Document (SDD) is a detailed blueprint describing a systemâ€™s architecture, components, data models, interfaces, and design decisions. It captures _how_ the software will be built before implementation begins.

---
### `Key Ideas`

- Defines system structure at both high and low levels.
    
- Describes modules, components, and their interactions.
    
- Includes diagrams such as architecture, UML, ERD, and sequences.
    
- Communicates design decisions, constraints, and rationale.
    
- Acts as a reference for developers, testers, and stakeholders.
